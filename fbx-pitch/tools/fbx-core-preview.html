Factory Standard Design
One spec across the network

The standard design is the contract between Core, the suppliers, and the franchise. It defines:

  • Panel sizes and connection details
  • Pod interfaces (plumbing, electrical, HVAC stub-outs)
  • Tolerance bands for site-built work
  • QA acceptance criteria

When the standard updates, every Home-Fab updates. Versioning is centralized.

Bath / Kitchen Pods
Pre-built wet modules

The plumbing-heavy parts of the home are built in a controlled factory environment, tested under pressure, and dropped into the panel envelope on site.

Why pods

  • Wet-trade work is the highest-defect zone in conventional construction
  • Factory pressure-test catches what site inspection misses
  • Compresses schedule by ~3 weeks per home

Receiving
Domestic &amp; foreign supply consolidation

The Core warehouse is the consolidation point. Inbound mix:

  • Containers — foreign-sourced panels and pods, port-to-DC
  • Truckload — domestic suppliers, direct-to-DC
  • Dropship-eligible — long-lead items routed straight to Home-Fabs

Receiving runs QA on every inbound: dimensional checks, moisture, finish, manifest match. Failed lots are rejected at the dock, not at the field.

This is where panels and pods become a kit. Each home order is staged on its own pallet sequence: walls, roof, pods, finish components, fasteners, drawings.

The kit ships out as a ready-to-assemble package, in the order it'll be installed. No on-site sorting, no missing parts, no return trips.

Proposed (from fbx-core-cards.json)
Sorting
Create the kit of parts

Receives bulk inventory, picks against the master spec, builds a complete Kit-of-Parts (~600 SKUs per kit) per home order. Each kit is a containerized shipment — single-touch from sort floor to outbound truck. Quality gate: every 20th kit gets a full-line audit by QA. Tooling roadmap: barcode-driven pick paths in Year 1-3, then AS/RS automation in Years 4-7 to absorb volume growth without linear FTE growth.

Quality Assurance
Cross-Fab consistency

QA happens at three points: at the supplier (specification compliance), at Core receiving (manifest and dimensional), and at the Home-Fab (assembly fit-up).

Each home gets a digital QA dossier with photos, measurements, and signoffs. The dossier is the warranty audit trail and the data feed for continuous improvement.
