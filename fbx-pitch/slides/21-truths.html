The Operational Layers — Honestly

The unsexy parts that actually matter.

What We're Not Hand-Waving

A constraint-aware design platform is the wedge. But it only matters if four operational layers actually work. None are research problems. All are solvable. None should be hand-waved.

Truth 01

Code & stamping.

Output has to be stamped by licensed engineers, jurisdiction by jurisdiction. Not a feature flag — a real operations layer with PE partnerships in each target state.

Approach
Start in 3–5 permissive states. Expand on demand.
Truth 02

Manufacturer abstraction.

Each SIP manufacturer has its own panel specs, splines, and tolerances. One design file → 2–3 manufacturer quotes. The kit-of-parts standardization the industry never bothered to build.

Approach
Clean abstraction layer · per-mfr config
Truth 03

Transport & crane planning.

A design that's perfect on paper but needs a crane the local rental yard doesn't carry is a failed design. The constraint solver has to know logistics, not just geometry.

Approach
Site-aware solver · regional equipment lookups
Truth 04

Builder network.

Even a perfect kit needs a crew that can erect it. The Last Mile franchise (slides 06, 11) is exactly this layer — vetted installers and GCs who learn the system and own the customer relationship.

Approach
Already in the deck · Last Mile network
Framing
None of these are research problems. All are solvable with operations rigor and a focused rollout. This is what we've already started building.
